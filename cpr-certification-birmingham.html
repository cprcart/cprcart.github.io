ACLS Classes

For advanced medical professionals, ACLS Classes (Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support) provide in-depth training on managing cardiac and respiratory emergencies. These classes build on BLS skills and focus on:
  • Recognizing and managing cardiac arrest and stroke
  • Team-based resuscitation dynamics
  • Advanced airway management
  • Pharmacology for life-threatening conditions
ACLS certification is often required for paramedics, nurses, physicians, and critical care staff. Birmingham offers AHA-accredited ACLS training programs designed to meet hospital and professional requirements.

BLS Classes

BLS Classes (Basic Life Support) are the foundation of emergency care. Required for most healthcare workers, these classes teach high-quality CPR techniques for single rescuers and teams. You’ll learn to:
  • Perform chest compressions and ventilations correctly
  • Use automated external defibrillators (AEDs)
  • Recognize life-threatening emergencies quickly
Birmingham’s BLS certification courses follow the latest American Heart Association guidelines and often include both initial certification and renewal options.

PALS Classes

If you work with infants or children, PALS Classes (Pediatric Advanced Life Support) are essential. These courses prepare healthcare professionals to manage pediatric emergencies like cardiac arrest, respiratory distress, and shock. In PALS certification classes, you’ll cover:
  • Pediatric assessment and early intervention
  • Effective resuscitation techniques
  • Team coordination and communication
  • Advanced airway management in infants and children
Hospitals and pediatric care facilities in Birmingham often require PALS certification for nurses, doctors, and emergency personnel.
